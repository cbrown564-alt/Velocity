# Story Quality Checklist And Action-Title Rubric

Apply this to a generated deck before claiming report-quality progress.

## Deck Story

- [ ] Audience and job are stated.
- [ ] Executive summary follows Situation-Complication-Resolution.
- [ ] Sections have a clear purpose and order.
- [ ] Each slide has one message.
- [ ] Recommendations are separated from evidence.
- [ ] Caveats appear where a reviewer will see them.
- [ ] Repeated charts or near-duplicate slides are removed.

## Action Titles

Score each title 0-3.

| Score | Standard |
| :--- | :--- |
| 0 | Topic label, unsupported claim, or misleading conclusion. |
| 1 | States a direction but is vague, too long, or only partly supported. |
| 2 | Complete sentence, specific, supported by the slide, and under 15 words. |
| 3 | Quantified, decisive, supports the section story, and survives title-only reading. |

Checks:

- [ ] Conclusion, not topic.
- [ ] 15 words or fewer.
- [ ] Uses active voice.
- [ ] Carries direction and magnitude when available.
- [ ] Every claim traces to visible or inspectable data.
- [ ] On wave refresh, title changes are flagged for confirmation.

## Promotion Notes

Narrative cannot be averaged away. If the deck has no SCR summary or most slides use topic titles, narrative score is 1 or lower even if the PPTX is technically clean.
