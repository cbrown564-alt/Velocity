**Target Date:** Q4 2027 **Vision Statement:** "From Data Processor to Research Partner." **Core Shift:** Moving from **"User asks Question -> Tool gives Answer"** to **"Tool observes Data -> Tool suggests Insight."**

## 1. The Core Philosophy: "The Living Insight System"

In the End State, Velocity is no longer just a file reader. It is a persistent, multiplayer environment where Quantitative (Surveys) and Qualitative (Interviews/Video) data live together in a unified "Knowledge Graph."

- **The MVP was:** A fast calculator for `.SAV` files.
    
- **The Vision is:** An autonomous research department in a box.

## 2. The Four Pillars of the Future

### Pillar A: The "Analyst Agents" (Autonomous Discovery)

_Current State:_ You have to know what to ask (e.g., "Filter by Age < 30"). _Future State:_ The system proactively tells you what matters.

- **Feature: The "Morning Briefing"**
    
    - When Sarah logs in, she doesn't see a blank canvas. She sees a feed:
        
    - _"I noticed that **NPS dropped 5 points** among 'Urban Millennials' last night. This correlates with the negative sentiment in the open-ended comments about 'Shipping Costs'."_
        
    - **Tech:** Background agents run thousands of permutations (Crosstabs) overnight to find significant anomalies.
        
- **Feature: The "Unasked Question" Engine**
    
    - As Sarah builds a table on "Brand Awareness," a subtle "Insight Chip" appears:
        
    - _"Did you know? If you filter this by 'Income > $100k', the story flips completely."_
        
    - **Tech:** Heuristic AI that checks for "Simpson's Paradox" or hidden variables in real-time.
        

### Pillar B: "Qual/Quant Fusion" (The Unified Field Theory)

_Current State:_ Survey data is in SPSS. Interview transcripts are in Word/Dovetail. They never talk. _Future State:_ A single drag-and-drop canvas for Numbers and Narratives.

- **Feature: The "Why" Overlay**
    
    - Sarah sees a bar chart showing **"Satisfaction dropped to 20%."**
        
    - She clicks the bar.
        
    - _Interaction:_ The bar expands to reveal **video clips** of real respondents from that specific segment explaining _why_ they are unhappy.
        
    - **Tech:** Vector database linking survey IDs to transcript timestamps.
        
- **Feature: Universal Search**
    
    - Search query: _"Why do people hate the new packaging?"_
        
    - _Result:_ A generated paragraph synthesizing **500 survey comments**, **20 interview transcripts**, and **5 video clips**, with citations linked to the raw data.
        

### Pillar C: "Synthetic Rehearsal" (The Digital Twin)

_Current State:_ You launch a survey and pray you asked the right questions. _Future State:_ You test your survey on a "Synthetic Audience" first.

- **Feature: The "Ghost Panel"**
    
    - Before spending $20k on a real field study, Sarah uploads her questionnaire.
        
    - Velocity spins up **1,000 Synthetic Respondents** (based on her previous 5 years of proprietary data) to simulate answers.
        
    - _Output:_ "Warning: Question 4 is confusing to synthetic 'Seniors'. You might get bad data here."
        
- **Feature: "What If" Modeling**
    
    - _"What would happen to our Net Promoter Score if we fixed the 'Shipping' issue?"_
        
    - The system adjusts the weight of that attribute and predicts the new topline score based on regression models.
        

### Pillar D: "The Data Living Room" (Multiplayer Storytelling)

_Current State:_ You paste static charts into PowerPoint. If the data changes, the deck is dead. _Future State:_ The "Presentation" is just a view of the live app.

- **Feature: The "Live Deck"**
    
    - Sarah sends a link to the client. It looks like a slide deck.
        
    - _Client:_ "What about Gen Z?"
        
    - _Sarah:_ She doesn't say "I'll get back to you." She toggles a filter on the slide _during the presentation_. The charts update instantly.
        
- **Feature: "Cursor Chat" on Data Points**
    
    - The Client highlights a weird outlier on the scatterplot.
        
    - They type: _"Is this a data error?"_
        
    - Sarah (or the AI Agent) replies right there on the chart.

## 3. The New User Journey (A Day in 2027)

1. **09:00 AM - The Ingestion:** Sarah drags a zip file containing 5,000 survey rows and 20 hours of Zoom interviews into Velocity.
    
2. **09:05 AM - The Auto-Structure:** Velocity parses the `.SAV`, auto-transcribes the video, and creates a "Linked Data Model." It identifies that Respondent #45 in the survey is the same person as "Dave" in the video interview.
    
3. **10:00 AM - The Exploration:** Sarah asks the AI: _"Build a profile of our 'At Risk' customers."_
    
    - Velocity builds a "Canvas" with:
        
        - A Crosstab of Demographics (Quant).
            
        - A Word Cloud of their complaints (Qual).
            
        - A Highlight Reel of 3 users crying about the price (Video).
            
4. **02:00 PM - The "Agent" Nudge:** While Sarah is staring at the data, the **"Analyst Agent"** pings: _"I found a strong correlation between 'Time on Site' and 'Churn' that you haven't looked at yet."_
    
5. **04:00 PM - The Client Huddle:** Sarah shares the "Live Deck." They co-edit the story in real-time. No emails. No "v2_final_FINAL.pptx."

## 4. Technical Moat (Why IBM Can't Copy This)

1. **The "Proprietary Knowledge Graph":** We don't just store rows and columns. We store the _relationships_between a "rating of 2" and the "text comment" that explains it.
    
2. **Local-First Privacy:** Even with all this AI, we stick to our "Local-First" roots for sensitive data. We use **Small Language Models (SLMs)** that run directly in the browser (WASM) to analyze text without sending PII to OpenAI.
    
3. **The "Taxonomy Engine":** Over time, Velocity learns the specific language of the client's industry (e.g., learning that "SKU" means "Product" in retail but something else in telecom).

## 5. Immediate Action: The Bridge to the Future

To get here, we don't build "Agents" today. We build the **Infrastructure** that allows Agents to exist later.

- **Step 1 (The MVP):** Build the fast `.SAV` reader (Velocity v1).
    
- **Step 2 (The Data Layer):** Ensure every table cell has a unique ID so an AI can reference it later.
    
- **Step 3 (The Text Layer):** Add a simple "Open End" viewer next to the crosstabs.
    

This vision moves Velocity from being a **"Better Calculator"** to being a **"Digital Colleague."**
